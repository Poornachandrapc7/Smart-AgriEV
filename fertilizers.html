Fertilizers Details

Urea : Urea is the most commonly used nitrogen-based fertilizer in India. It contains 46% nitrogen, making it highly effective in promoting vegetative growth and overall plant strength. Urea is affordable and widely used for a variety of crops, especially cereals like wheat and rice.

NPK fertilizers : NPK fertilizers are complex fertilizers containing varying amounts of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. They are tailored to meet the specific nutrient requirements of crops and are widely used in rice, wheat, cotton, and vegetables.

NP and PK fertilizers : NP and PK fertilizers contain nitrogen and phosphorus (NP) or phosphorus and potassium (PK). These fertilizers are ideal for crops that require only two of the three essential nutrients and are used for more targeted fertilization.

Ammonium Sulfate : Ammonium Sulfate contains 21% nitrogen and 24% sulfur. It is used for crops like oilseeds and pulses that need both sulfur and nitrogen. It also helps to reduce the pH level of alkaline soils, making it ideal for basic soils.

Calcium Ammonium Nitrate (CAN) : CAN is a top-dressing fertilizer containing 26% nitrogen. It provides quick-release nitrogen, making it suitable for crops needing a short-term boost. It helps in improving plant health and is typically applied to crops like vegetables and potatoes.

Single Super Phosphate (SSP) : SSP is primarily composed of calcium and sulfur and contains 16-20% phosphorus. It is widely used for crops like oilseeds, lentils, and legumes. It promotes strong root development and improves the overall health of the plant.

Diammonium Phosphate (DAP) : DAP is a widely used phosphorus fertilizer containing 46% phosphorus and 18% nitrogen. It is most effective during planting as it ensures strong early growth and development of the plant, especially for crops like wheat and maize.

Muriate of Potash (MOP) : MOP is a potassium fertilizer containing 60-62% potassium. It is commonly used for a variety of crops, including cereals, vegetables, and fruits. It helps in improving disease resistance, water regulation, and overall plant health.

Potassium Sulfate (SOP) : SOP contains 18% sulfur and 50% potassium. It is used in crops sensitive to chloride, such as potatoes, tobacco, and some fruits. It is also effective in controlling chloride levels in saline soils.
