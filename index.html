Battery Care Guidelines

Healthy Battery Status ✅

  • Stable Voltage:

    The battery maintains a stable voltage range of 13.5V – 13.8V in standby mode and 14.4V – 15.0V in cycle mode.

  • Minimal Self-Discharge:

    The battery holds a charge for weeks/months when not in use.

  • Low Heat:

    The battery remains cool during usage and charging.

  • Clean Terminals:

    Terminals are free from corrosion or rust.

  • Physical Condition:

    No swelling, cracks, or leaks are observed in the battery casing.

  • Charging Current:

    The battery draws below 0.39A during charging as per manufacturer specifications.

  • Consistent Performance:

    The battery provides consistent power output with no sudden voltage drops.

Needs Maintenance Status ⚠️

Signs that your battery needs maintenance or replacement:

  • Voltage Drop:

    Standby voltage falls below 13.5V or cycle voltage drops significantly.

  • Quick Discharge:

    The battery drains unusually fast under normal usage.

  • Excessive Heating:

    The battery heats up during charging or operation.

  • Corroded Terminals:

    White powdery residue or rust appears around battery terminals.

  • Physical Damage:

    Swelling, cracks, or leaks are visible in the battery casing.

  • Irregular Charging:

    The battery takes too long to charge or stops charging prematurely.

  • Unstable Performance:

    The battery experiences significant voltage drops under load or fluctuates in output.
